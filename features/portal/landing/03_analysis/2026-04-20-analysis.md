# Analysis Pack

## Requirement Interpretation

The landing module has two jobs at the same time:

- persuade the user by explaining benefits
- reduce onboarding friction by clarifying application dossier preparation

This means the module is both an entry page and a preparation guide, not just a marketing banner. The content has to support conversion without sacrificing preparation clarity.

## Current vs Target Gap View

| Area | Current Risk if Undefined | Target State |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Benefit messaging | Users do not understand why they should continue | The landing module communicates a concise value proposition in one scan |
| Dossier preparation guidance | Users start without knowing what to prepare | The landing module sets clear preparation expectations before registration |
| CTA routing | Users are unsure whether to register or log in | `Sign up` and `Log in` paths are both obvious and easy to access |
| Traceability | Requirements are hard to validate downstream | Acceptance criteria and UAT scenarios are linked back to source requirements |

## Key Dependency Areas

### Content Dependency

- Benefit statements must be confirmed by product or business owner.
- Application dossier preparation guidance must be confirmed by the operational process owner.

### Navigation Dependency

- `Sign up` destination must be defined.
- `Log in` destination must be defined.

### Measurement Dependency

- Product analytics must define CTA tracking events and success metrics.

## Decision Points Requiring Confirmation

- Whether the landing module shows a summarized dossier checklist or only high-level preparation guidance.
- Whether different user segments require different dossier instructions.
- Whether `Sign up` should be visually primary over `Log in`.
- Whether any compliance, legal, or disclaimer content must appear near the preparation guidance.

## Risks

| Risk ID | Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| RISK-01 | Benefit messaging is too generic or inconsistent with the actual service. | Reduced trust and conversion | Validate copy with business owner before build |
| RISK-02 | Preparation guidance is incomplete or inconsistent with the actual dossier requirements. | Users start the process unprepared and drop off later | Confirm canonical dossier list before release |
| RISK-03 | The page tries to explain too much. | Lower readability and weaker CTA conversion | Keep summary content concise and move detail to secondary help if needed |
| RISK-04 | CTA hierarchy is unclear. | User confusion and lower conversion | Confirm primary CTA strategy and content layout |
| RISK-05 | Mobile presentation compresses content in a way that hides one of the CTAs or makes preparation guidance hard to scan. | Reduced conversion or misunderstandings on smaller screens | Validate responsive layout and scannable content behavior during design and UAT |

## Recommendation

- Treat this as a mandatory onboarding entry module.
- Prioritize clarity over marketing density.
- Keep dossier preparation guidance action-oriented and easy to scan.
- Ensure CTA labels are immediately visible without requiring the user to interpret ambiguous wording.
- Use requirement-to-UAT traceability so that benefit messaging, preparation guidance, and CTA routing are all testable.

## Suggested Next BA Actions

- Confirm exact dossier list and preparation rules.
- Confirm whether there are segment-specific document requirements.
- Confirm information architecture for benefits, preparation guidance, and CTA placement.
- Define acceptance criteria with UX and product for visibility, scannability, and clarity of both CTAs.
